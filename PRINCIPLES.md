# Principles

## 1. AI should amplify thinking, not replace it

I am interested in AI that improves judgment, speed, and clarity, not AI that encourages intellectual laziness.

## 2. Real systems matter more than demos

A flashy prototype is easy. A tool that someone can actually use, trust, and maintain is more valuable.

## 3. Human-in-the-loop is a feature

For meaningful work, oversight is not a weakness. Review, approval, and iteration are part of building responsibly.

## 4. Practical beats performative

The best AI work is often quiet: fewer clicks, faster diagnosis, better organization, clearer decisions, less friction.

## 5. Infrastructure and AI belong together

A lot of valuable AI work happens at the layer of systems, workflows, tooling, and operations, not just in polished chat interfaces.

## 6. Public work should stay clean

Anything shared publicly should avoid secrets, unnecessary infrastructure exposure, and careless operational details.
